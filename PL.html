(My opinions are marked with underscore.)

Type systems rule out programs that does not "make sense".

Programs that are not ruled out by a type system are well-typed. To put it another way, every well-typed program "makes sense".

Every good type system is in a Curry-Howard Correspondence with a logic.

Practical Fundations of Programming Languages gives nice definitions of many things.

Types and Programming Languages is another good book for looking up definitions.

The Lambda Calculus is untyped.

Many untyped programming languages are actually unityped -- there is only one type and the language is type-safe.

Type-safety stands for progression and preservation.

Progression in type-safety means every well-typed program is either a value or reduces to another program.

Preservation in type-safety means whenever a well-typed program reduces to a program, the target program is also well-typed.

Church encoding means using lambda abstractions to represent everything.

Church encoding is the only "right" way to specify the semantics of product and sum.

Product is also known as Pair.

Multiarity product is known as tuple. Labeled product is also known as record.

Multiarity sum has no other name. Labeled sum is also known as variant.
